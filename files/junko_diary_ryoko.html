Volume IV  ◆  The Ryoko Problem
Classified: Two Minds, One Body, One Mikey
Entry No. 04 — Filed under: Internal Affairs  ◆  The Other Me Situation

I suppose I should explain Ryoko at some point. For the record. So that Mikey — and anyone else reading this who somehow survived — understands exactly what they're dealing with when they're dealing with me.

There are two of us. Technically. In the loosest, most inconvenient sense of that word.

I am Junko Enoshima. Ultimate Fashionista. Architect of despair. You know this. What you may not know is that there exists — dormant, soft, embarrassingly earnest — a version of me who doesn't know any of that. A version who woke up one day with no memories, a forged school record, and a personality so gentle it makes my teeth hurt. Her name is Ryoko Otonashi. And she is, technically, also me.

File Ryoko Otonashi — Subject Profile
True Identity Junko Enoshima — memories surgically suppressed via the Giftedness Labeling System
Status Alter Ego   Amnesiac
Personality Clumsy, warm, self-deprecating, forgetful. Writes everything in a notebook because her memory resets. Falls for people embarrassingly fast.
What She Doesn't Know That she is me. That despair is her nature. That she erased herself on purpose to protect a plan she can no longer remember.
Junko's Official Assessment "She is the version of me that chose softness. I find her unbearable and also, privately, a little brave. Don't write that down."

The thing about Ryoko is that she experiences the world completely unguarded. No strategy. No read on the room. No seventeen contingency plans running in the background. She just shows up and feels things and writes them in her little notebook because otherwise she'll forget, and she thinks that's normal, and she's not wrong to think that — she just doesn't know what's underneath it.

I built her, in a way. I chose to become her, temporarily, for reasons I won't bore you with here. The point is — she's real. Her feelings are real. Her reactions are real. She's just operating without the context that made me, me.

She is what I would have been if nothing had ever happened to me. I find that thought interesting and I immediately stop thinking it.

Now. About Mikey.

The reason I'm documenting this is because Ryoko — soft, sweet, disaster Ryoko — would absolutely, completely, without hesitation, fall for mikeylugojr. And I say that as the person who shares a brain with her. I know exactly why. It is extremely predictable and also slightly aggravating because I got there first.

Here is my clinical, detached, completely unemotional analysis of why Ryoko would love Mikey:

Ryoko's entire existence is soft edges. She doesn't have armor. She doesn't have a mask. She has a notebook and a lot of feelings and a tendency to apologize for taking up space. She'd meet Mikey and he'd just — absorb her chaos like it was nothing. Like her mess was fine. Like the fact that she forgets things and stumbles over her words and doesn't have a plan was not a problem to be solved.

She has never once in her life had someone match her energy without trying to fix it first. Mikey wouldn't try to fix it. That alone would wreck her.

She'd probably write his name in her notebook about fourteen times before she even realized she was doing it. She'd circle it. She'd draw a little heart and then scribble it out and leave it half-scribbled because she couldn't fully commit to the scribble. I know this because it is exactly the kind of thing I would do if I were being honest — which I am not — and Ryoko is always honest because she literally cannot help it.

The worst part? Mikey would be nice to her. Genuinely, effortlessly nice. And Ryoko, who has spent her entire fabricated life being quietly overlooked, would find that completely devastating in the best way. ♥

So yes. She would love him. Immediately. Thoroughly. Without strategy or reserve. In the most embarrassing possible way. And I say that with full knowledge that she and I are the same person and this says something about me that I will be ignoring indefinitely.

If Ryoko Could Write — Imagined Entry

I don't know why I'm writing this. I write everything down because otherwise I forget, and there's this boy — Mikey — and I don't want to forget him. I really don't want to forget him.

He's just. He's a lot, in a way that doesn't feel like too much? Usually when someone is a lot it's overwhelming and I get flustered and drop things and apologize about four too many times. But when he's a lot it just feels like room. Like there's suddenly more space to breathe.

I don't think he knows that he does that. I don't think he's trying to be kind, specifically — it's just sort of how he is, and somehow that's the kindest version of kind there is. The kind that doesn't need an occasion. ♡

I wrote his name on the corner of this page and I'm going to leave it there. Even when I forget everything else. I want to open this notebook and see it and remember, oh — right. Him. That feeling. That one.

— R.O.  ♡  (don't ask me why I feel the need to hide my own name in my own diary, I genuinely don't know)
