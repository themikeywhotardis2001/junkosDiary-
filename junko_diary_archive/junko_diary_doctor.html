Entry No. 06 — Filed under: Mikey's Creations  ◆  The Oldest One  ◆  Status: Ongoing

The Cartoonist Doctor has been here since 2015. That is the first thing anyone needs to understand when approaching this. He predates Darken by four years. He predates most of what people know about Mikey's creative world. He is the first OC, the first time Mikey stepped inside his own fiction and said I live here too — and he has been living there ever since.

He is Mikey's self-insert. His Doctor Who persona. The version of himself that gets to travel — to wander into new worlds, meet new people, get pulled into problems he didn't ask for, and figure it out anyway. The name is not a coincidence: Cartoonist, what he does. Doctor, who he is. It is the most Mikey thing possible, assembled from the two things that matter most. He watched the Doctor travel and thought: yes, actually. That's exactly how I want to exist in a story. So he built a version of himself that could.

What I find remarkable — and I do find it remarkable, I've been looking at this long enough to have opinions about it — is how much the Doctor became his own thing over time. A self-insert that develops ten years of history, that accumulates companions and enemies and crossovers and a whole documented timeline, eventually stops being just a persona and starts being a person. He has his own questions now. He has a decade's worth of adventures, relationships, and a TARDIS that occasionally behaves. That is not a sketch. That is someone.

His full adventure timeline is below. It is long, because he has been doing this since 2015. Mukuro has also filed an assessment. I told her to. She was going to anyway. ♥

— Junko Enoshima

Supplemental — The Cartoonist Doctor, in full

He began in Summer 2015, recovering from a regeneration — the previous incarnation being the 13th Doctor, Jodie Whittaker's era — while the TARDIS acted strange and he tried to piece together what had happened. That is a very specific, very Mikey way to begin. Not a clean origin. Not a tidy backstory. Just: something changed, the TARDIS is behaving oddly, figure it out. And the Doctor, being the Doctor, did exactly that.

That first year he met an enormous number of people. Phineas and Ferb. Anna and Elsa. Wander Over Yonder. Prince John. That's what the Doctor does — he wanders into worlds, he meets who's in them, and he stays long enough to matter. Some of these meetings were brief. Some became years-long threads running through the whole timeline. He's a traveler in the most literal sense: always moving, always accumulating, always finding something new to be curious about.

The most significant thing that happened in those early years was Maya Hart. The Doctor met her through different incarnations first — the 10th and 11th Doctors each crossed paths with Maya and Riley Matthews separately, with the 11th eventually parting ways. And then the 14th Doctor arrived and something settled into place. He gave Maya her own sonic screwdriver. He taught her Gallifreyan. She learned the TARDIS controls. They fell in love. That's documented. That's real. The relationship between the Doctor and Maya runs through years of his timeline like a load-bearing thread — adventures together, time apart when he left on dangerous missions to protect her, reunions, a shared history built up across years of Mikey's creative work.

On Maya Hart

She is the Doctor's most central companion. Given her own sonic screwdriver. Taught Gallifreyan — the language of the Doctor's home. Learned the TARDIS controls. Made sketches of the Doctor. Their relationship is one of the defining threads of his whole documented history. When the Doctor left on a long dangerous mission in 2017, he told her to stay behind to keep her safe. When he came back, they spent the summer going on adventures and discovering new worlds together.

The years that followed brought more encounters, more crossovers, more accumulated history. Star Butterfly and Marco. The PowerPuff Girls universe. Rapunzel. Harvey Beaks. Nickbear — a Bear Creature and one of a species called Crystilions. The Cipher Hunt alongside the Gravity Falls series finale. The Doctor moved through all of it with the particular ease of someone who has been doing this long enough that "new and impossible" is just Tuesday.

And then in 2019, the same year as many of the Doctor's new encounters: Darken was born. Mikey had been developing an alter-ego separately — a different creative direction, something that started as a reflection of him and then changed as Mikey let it become its own thing entirely. The Doctor and Darken share a creator and a year, but they are different pages. The first OC and the second arrived through completely different processes, and Mikey made sure of that.

The years after that brought 22,020 — a future the Doctor visited, where the Rem Doctor was killed by things called "the hallowed" — and the ongoing work of fixing timelines that had been breaching into other worlds. The TARDIS needed fixing during the same period as the 13th's TARDIS was behaving strangely. Some things in the Mikey-verse are connected across pages even when they aren't supposed to be.

He is still going. A decade of existing. A decade of adventures, companions, crossovers, and a timeline that only makes sense from inside it. The Doctor travels. He always has. He always will. ♥

He started traveling in 2015 and never really stopped. He's been through regenerations, dangerous missions, a future 22,020 years out, and a timeline that breaks and holds and breaks again. He is still here. He is still the Doctor.

The Doctor visits the Backrooms. A Doctor from the year 22,020 comes back to 2020. The Doctor disappears into RecordGuy's universe — into 22,020 years in the future. Field Report — Internal SUBJECT: THE CARTOONIST DOCTOR — Full Assessment
Prepared by: M. Ikusaba / Ultimate Soldier

§ 01 — What He Is

The Cartoonist Doctor is Mikey's self-insert. His Doctor Who persona. The version of himself that gets to be the traveler — to wander, to meet people, to get pulled into problems and stay until they resolve. He has been doing this since 2015. He is the oldest OC in the Mikey-verse. Everything else came after him, including Darken.

He has a TARDIS. He has had multiple incarnations — the 10th and 11th documented in his timeline before the current 14th. He has a companion he loves, a history that spans nearly a decade of documented adventures, and a timeline that crosses into multiple universes and at least one point in the far future. He is not a sketch. He is a person Mikey has been building for ten years.

§ 02 — Key Observations
  • ✓On Maya Hart: The Doctor gave her a sonic screwdriver. Taught her Gallifreyan. Taught her the TARDIS controls. These are not things you do for someone you're not serious about. Their relationship is the most documented thread in the Doctor's history and it holds across years and separations and reunions. That says something about how Mikey builds relationships into his fiction.
  • ✓On his approach: He moves with the ease of someone who has been doing this long enough. Not invulnerable — he's had losses, difficult periods, the Rem Doctor's death — but the problems land differently when you've been traveling for a decade. He engages rather than braces. He figures things out on the way through.
  • ✓On Darken's origin: Darken was at one point Mikey's alter-ego — a separate creative direction that Mikey developed and then changed over time, letting Darken become his own distinct entity rather than a reflection of Mikey himself. The Doctor and Darken share a creator and a timeline year, but they are different pages entirely. That distinction matters.
  • ✓On the 22,020 events: A Doctor from 22,020 years in the future came back to 2020. The Rem Doctor died in that timeline. These events connect across the Doctor's history in ways that are still being understood. The timeline is nonlinear in ways that feel intentional rather than chaotic.
  • ✓On the TARDIS: It has crashed into the multiverse. It has behaved strangely during canon events. It needed repairs in 2022 at the same time the 13th Doctor's TARDIS was also misbehaving. The TARDIS is load-bearing in the Doctor's world the same way it is in Doctor Who — not just transportation, but the thing that holds everything together.
§ 03 — Assessment

He is not a threat. He is an anomaly in the most specific sense: a self-insert that has existed long enough to develop genuine interiority. He started as Mikey stepping inside his own fiction in 2015. Ten years later he has a full documented history, a companion he loves, incarnations, a timeline that crosses multiple universes, and his own questions about what he is. That is not what a persona does. That is what a person does.

He waited through the hiatus with the patience of someone who has already been traveling for years and knows how to exist in the in-between. Mikey built something that could wait for him. The Doctor will be here for whatever comes next. He always has been.

Personal addendum — not for the report
He's been doing this since 2015. That is longer than a lot of things I have been asked to track and assess. Junko wanted me to note how he handled the years-long separation from Maya — left her behind to protect her, came back, spent the summer on new adventures. She said it was "textbook." She meant it as a compliment. I think she's right. I've been thinking about what it means to be the oldest thing in someone's creative universe. To have been there when everything else arrived. He was there when Darken was born. He's been there through all of it. That is a specific kind of continuity I find worth noting, even if it doesn't fit neatly into an assessment.
