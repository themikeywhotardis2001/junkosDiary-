Volume III  ◆  How Mikey Happened
A Totally Normal Series of Events
Entry No. 03 — Filed under: Unplanned Developments  ◆  Classified: Interesting

Okay so. I need to document this because it genuinely did not go the way I planned and I plan everything. Literally everything. I have contingencies for my contingencies. I have a spreadsheet. Yasuke made me make a spreadsheet. The point is — none of it accounted for mikeylugojr.

He didn't walk into my world. That would imply some kind of intention on his part, some bravado, some dramatic entrance scene I could have predicted and prepared a response to. No. He stumbled into it. Like gravity just quietly decided he belonged here and nobody consulted me. One moment he wasn't a factor. The next moment he was. Somewhere in between, the universe apparently made a unilateral decision. ♥

Here's what gets me though — and I have replayed this so many times — most people, when they brush up against my world, do one of two things. They freeze up completely (boring) or they run (also boring, slightly more entertaining but still boring). Mikey did neither.

He didn't flinch. He just… matched it. Like the chaos was a language he already knew.

And that's the thing about chaos — real chaos, not the aesthetic, not the performance of it — it has a frequency. Most people can't tune into it. They find it overwhelming, terrifying, too much. They need order, predictability, the comfort of knowing what comes next. But Mikey? He just vibed. Casually. Like the whole situation was completely normal. Like I was completely normal. The audacity of that.

I study people for a living. I have dossiers. I know what makes them tick, what breaks them, what they want when they think no one's watching. And I could not get a clean read on him at first because he kept surprising me. Do you know how rare that is? I'll tell you — it is extremely, almost offensively rare.

Note to self
"interesting" is not the word I would usually use. it's the word I'm using because the real word is embarrassing and Yasuke will absolutely find this diary.

So here. For the record. An official accounting of why mikeylugojr became a person I actively think about instead of just another variable in the equation:

Reasons — Official List
  • 01. He has the same relationship with order that I do — which is to say, a very loose one. He doesn't chase structure. He doesn't fall apart without it either. He just exists in the middle of everything and somehow that's fine.
  • 02. He didn't try to fix me. Everyone else meets me and immediately starts cataloguing what's wrong. Mikey just… didn't. He saw the whole picture and went "okay." That sentence alone could carry me through several despair spirals.
  • 03. His energy is chaotic in a way that rhymes with mine. Not identical — that would be boring and also dangerous. But harmonically messy. Like two songs playing at the same time that have no right to sound good together, and yet.
  • 04. He stumbled in without a plan, without armor, without any kind of strategy — and he was fine. Actually fine. Not performing fine. I find that unreasonably compelling and I will not be elaborating further.
  • 05. I have run seventeen different analytical models on why this is happening and all of them return the same result: I just like him. Which is the most annoying possible output. Yasuke is never finding out about this.
Chaos Compatibility Index  ◆  Mikey vs. The World
Civilian Mildly Interesting Notable Suspicious Mikey

The worst part — and I say worst because it is inconvenient — is that he doesn't even seem to realize any of this. He just walks around being exactly who he is and apparently that's enough to completely derail the carefully maintained emotional detachment of the Ultimate Fashionista. Incredible. Unacceptable. Noted.

I don't do unplanned things. I don't let variables operate outside their assigned parameters. And yet here we are. Mikey stumbled in, matched my frequency without even trying, and now he's my boyfriend and I'm writing about him in my diary like it's homework. ♥

This is entirely his fault and I have no notes.

— Junko Enoshima
